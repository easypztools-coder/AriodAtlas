BOTANICAL ACCURACY REQUIREMENTS

Critical identifying traits for Philodendron 'Caramel Marble':

- Deeply serrated or pinnately lobed leaf margins, resembling a jagged saw-tooth edge
- Highly variegated foliage featuring marbled, sectoral, and speckled patterns of caramel, orange, bronze, yellow, cream, and forest green
- Thick, leathery, coriaceous leaf texture that is stiff to the touch
- Glossy adaxial leaf finish reflecting studio light
- Long, sturdy reddish-brown or dark maroon petioles
- Elongated narrow-oblong to lanceolate leaf blade shape

Morphology:

Leaf Shape:
Deeply serrated to pinnately lobed margins with saw-tooth edges, lanceolate to narrow-oblong blade shape

Leaf Ratio:
Elongated leaves, approximately 4:1 to 6:1 length-to-width ratio (leaves 30–60 cm long × 8–15 cm wide)

Texture:
Thick, leathery (coriaceous), stiff, with a glossy waxy sheen on the adaxial surface

Petiole:
Long, sturdy, cylindrical, colored deep reddish-brown to dark green, slightly winged at the base

Growth Habit:
Slow-growing, self-heading when young, transitioning to a slow climbing/scandent habit with age, producing thick aerial roots

Mature Size:
Overall leaf length 30–60 cm; petioles 25–40 cm; mature height up to 1.2 meters when supported

Origin:
Cultivated selection/hybrid mutation, widely propagated in specialized collections globally.

Habitat:
Cultivated mutation originally selected from Philodendron hybridization. Thrives in high humidity (60–85%), warm temperatures (18–28°C), and bright indirect light to preserve and stimulate the colorful variegation patterns.

The specimen of Philodendron 'Caramel Marble' should immediately be recognizable to an experienced collector.

Failure to accurately reproduce species-defining morphology should be considered incorrect.
